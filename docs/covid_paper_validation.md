## PRELIMINARY FINDINGS: DRAMATIC CONTRASTS

### COVID Impact: Similar Health Crisis

**Cambodia**:
- 71% experienced serious/very serious impact
- Mean severity: 2.04 (closer to "serious")
- Only 3% said "not much impact"

**Thailand**:
- 43% experienced serious/very serious impact
- Mean severity: 2.45 (between "serious" and "some impact")
- Only 3% said "not much impact"

**Analysis**: Both countries experienced substantial COVID impact, with Cambodia hit slightly harder.

---

### Emergency Powers: HUGE DIVERGENCE

**Support for Emergency Powers During Health Crisis (q172a)**:
- **Cambodia: 86.5%** support (very/somewhat justified)
- **Thailand: 60.7%** support (very/somewhat justified)
- **Difference: 25.8 percentage points**

**Implication**: Despite similar health impacts, Cambodians are FAR more willing to accept emergency powers.

---

### Government COVID Response: MASSIVE GAP

**Trust Government Handled Pandemic Well (q142)**:
- **Cambodia: 93.6%** say handled well/very well
- **Thailand: 37.7%** say handled well/very well
- **Difference: 55.9 percentage points!**

**Implication**: This is the most dramatic difference in the data. Near-universal approval in Cambodia vs. strong disapproval in Thailand.

---

### Democracy Satisfaction: OPPOSITE TRAJECTORIES

**Satisfied with How Democracy Works (q90)**:
- **Cambodia: 87.8%** satisfied (very/fairly)
- **Thailand: 37.7%** satisfied (very/fairly)
- **Difference: 50.1 percentage points!**

**Implication**: Cambodia's authoritarian system enjoying high satisfaction; Thailand's democracy facing crisis of confidence.
